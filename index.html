Recognition & Awareness

Warning Signs of Addiction

Recognising these early signs is crucial to getting help before dependency becomes life-altering or fatal.

🧠
Compulsive Craving
Intense urges to use the substance that override rational thought, concentration, and daily functioning.
📉
Neglecting Responsibilities
Missing work, school, or family obligations. Loss of interest in hobbies and previously enjoyed activities.
🔺
Increasing Tolerance
Needing larger amounts to achieve the same effect as the body builds physiological resistance.
💸
Financial Problems
Unexplained money shortages, selling possessions, or regularly borrowing money with no explanation.
🔒
Withdrawal Symptoms
Physical or psychological distress — tremors, sweating, nausea, anxiety — when attempting to stop use.
👥
Social Withdrawal
Isolating from family, keeping secrets about activities, or changing friend groups to enable substance use.
😤
Extreme Mood Swings
Rapid swings between euphoria and severe irritability, depression, paranoia, or uncontrolled aggression.
🏥
Physical Decline
Rapid weight loss, dental decay, poor hygiene, bloodshot eyes, or extreme changes in energy and sleep.
🔁
Failed Quit Attempts
Repeatedly trying and failing to cut down or stop despite strong and sincere intent to do so.
⚠️
Reckless Behaviour
Engaging in dangerous activities under the influence, or putting oneself at risk to obtain the substance.
😴
Sleep Disruption
Severe insomnia or hypersomnia, erratic sleep schedules, or sleeping at unusual hours consistently.
🕵️
Secretive Behaviour
Hiding substances, lying about activities, going to unusual places, or becoming defensive when questioned.

Emergency Response

Overdose Response Guide

Knowing what to do during an overdose can save a life. Act immediately — every minute matters.

🚨 Call Emergency Services
  1. 1Call 999 (UK) / 911 (US) / 999 (BD) immediately
  2. 2State it is a drug overdose and substances taken if known
  3. 3Stay on the line — follow dispatcher instructions precisely
  4. 4Do not leave the person alone under any circumstances
💊 Opioid Overdose (Heroin/Fentanyl)
  1. 1Administer Naloxone (Narcan) nasal spray if available
  2. 2Place in recovery position if breathing (on side)
  3. 3Give rescue breaths if not breathing at all
  4. 4Repeat naloxone after 2–3 min if no response — fentanyl requires multiple doses
🫀 Stimulant Overdose (Cocaine/Meth)
  1. 1Keep the person calm and cool — overheating is lethal
  2. 2Apply cold wet cloths to neck, armpits, and groin
  3. 3Do NOT restrain — causes cardiac stress and injury
  4. 4If seizure: protect the head, time it, call EMS immediately
🔵 Depressant Overdose (Alcohol/Benzos/GHB)
  1. 1Never leave unconscious person face-up — choking risk
  2. 2Place in recovery position immediately (on their side)
  3. 3Monitor breathing every 2–3 minutes until EMS arrives
  4. 4Do NOT give coffee or attempt to "walk it off" — dangerous

Prevention & Recovery

How to Protect Yourself & Others

Prevention is achievable through awareness, strong community bonds, and the right support structures.

📚

Educate Yourself

Understanding addiction as a brain disease removes stigma and helps identify risks early. Knowledge is the single most powerful protective barrier.

🤝

Build a Support Network

Strong social bonds are the most protective factor against addiction. Surround yourself with people who support your wellbeing and to whom you can speak openly.

🧘

Manage Stress Healthily

Most substance use starts as a coping mechanism for stress. Exercise, mindfulness, creative outlets, and therapy are proven effective alternatives.

🗣️

Seek Help Early

If you or someone you know shows warning signs, act immediately. Early intervention dramatically improves recovery outcomes — don't wait for a crisis.

💊

Be Cautious with Prescriptions

Even prescription opioids and benzodiazepines carry serious addiction potential. Always follow dosage instructions and never share your medications.

🏡

Open Family Conversations

Children who can discuss drugs openly at home are far less likely to experiment. Provide facts, remove stigma, and keep the conversation ongoing.

🏋️

Physical Wellbeing

Regular exercise raises dopamine naturally, reducing the brain's need to seek artificial stimulation. Physical health is a powerful addiction prevention tool.

🔬

Test Before You Use

If substances are present in your environment, fentanyl test strips and drug checking services save lives by detecting deadly adulterants before use.
